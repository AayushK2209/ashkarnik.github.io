The Problem

Understanding where passenger demand has recovered — and where it hasn't — is a foundational question for any airline commercial team. The COVID-19 pandemic created a structural break in aviation demand patterns, making historical trend analysis both more complex and more important. Route-level visibility into passenger volumes, load factors, and frequency changes allows commercial teams to make informed capacity, pricing, and scheduling decisions.

This project builds a route-level demand dashboard using BITRE's Domestic Aviation Activity data, designed to deliver regular performance insight to a commercial stakeholder audience.

Data & Methodology

BITRE publishes monthly domestic aviation statistics at the route and carrier level, covering passengers carried, available seats, load factor, and aircraft movements. The dashboard ingests and transforms this data in Python before rendering stakeholder-facing views in Power BI and Tableau.

  • Python pipeline for data ingestion, cleaning, and metric calculation
  • Route-level load factor trends from 2019 through current period
  • Carrier comparison across Qantas, Virgin Australia, Jetstar, and Rex
  • COVID recovery index: current quarter vs 2019 equivalent baseline
  • Frequency and capacity trends by market segment (trunk, leisure, regional)

Leisure routes (particularly Gold Coast and Cairns) recovered passenger volume faster than trunk business corridors, but at structurally lower average fares — revealing a yield-volume trade-off that persists into 2024.

Dashboard Design

The dashboard is structured for two audiences: a summary view for senior commercial stakeholders (headline recovery metrics, route heat map, carrier share trend) and a drill-down view for analysts (route-level time series, load factor decomposition, frequency calendar). Both views are designed for regular refresh cadence as new BITRE data is published quarterly.

Commercial Implications

For a commercial performance or network planning team, this dashboard surfaces the routes where recovery is lagging (capacity restoration ahead of demand) and those where demand is outpacing current capacity — the latter representing potential yield optimisation opportunities. The framework is directly applicable to a Commercial Performance Analyst or Flight Analyst reporting function.
