Project 03 · BTS · Python

Airline Delay &
Disruption Cost Analysis

Dataset BTS On-Time Performance
Methods Cost driver modelling · Root cause attribution
Tools Python · pandas · matplotlib
Output Business recommendations

The Problem

Flight delays are among the most visible and costly operational failures in commercial aviation — affecting passenger experience, crew productivity, asset utilisation, and downstream schedule integrity. The US Bureau of Transportation Statistics classifies delays by cause: carrier, weather, National Aviation System (NAS), security, and late aircraft. Understanding the relative contribution of each category, and how it varies by carrier and route type, is foundational to operational improvement and cost control.

Data & Methodology

BTS On-Time Performance data provides flight-level delay information for US carriers, including cause category, delay minutes, and carrier-route identifiers. The analysis models delay cost drivers by:

  • Attributing delay minutes by cause category (carrier vs external) per carrier
  • Estimating operational cost exposure using published per-minute delay cost proxies
  • Identifying high-impact route-carrier combinations for targeted intervention
  • Comparing carrier-controllable vs non-controllable delay ratios as an operational discipline indicator

Carrier-attributable delays represent the largest controllable cost exposure — and vary significantly between carriers with similar route networks, suggesting operational rather than structural drivers.

Commercial Implications

For an operations or commercial performance team, this analysis identifies where investment in ground operations, turnaround efficiency, or crew scheduling would generate measurable cost reduction. The framing — as business recommendations rather than raw statistics — reflects the output expected of an analyst embedded in a commercial or operational function.
