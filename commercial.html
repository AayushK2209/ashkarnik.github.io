Project 05 · Tableau · Excel · Python

Customer & Commercial
Performance Analysis

Dataset 500+ user transactions
Key finding 25% spending uplift identified
Tools Tableau · Excel · Python
Output Stakeholder insight pack

The Problem

Loyalty and reward programmes are a significant commercial lever for airlines and retailers alike — but their effectiveness varies substantially by campaign design, customer segment, and timing. Measuring the incremental spend attributable to targeted promotions, rather than organic purchasing behaviour, requires a structured analytical approach that isolates programme impact from baseline trends.

Data & Methodology

Transaction and engagement data from 500+ programme participants was analysed across reward campaign periods. The methodology focused on:

  • Segmenting customers by engagement tier and baseline spend level
  • Isolating campaign-period transactions from non-campaign control windows
  • Calculating incremental spend uplift by promotion type and customer segment
  • Identifying which campaign structures drove the strongest behavioural response

Targeted promotions delivered a 25% spending uplift over baseline for engaged programme participants — with the effect concentrated in the mid-tier customer segment rather than high-frequency buyers.

Stakeholder Output

Findings were presented in a decision-focused format with clear implications for customer and product strategy — structured as a regular insight pack rather than a one-off report. This mirrors the cadence and format of portfolio-wide performance reporting typical of a commercial analytics function in freight or airline businesses.

Commercial Implications

The mid-tier concentration finding has direct strategic implications: maximising programme ROI means prioritising promotions aimed at growing mid-tier customers toward high-frequency behaviour, rather than over-investing in already-loyal top spenders. For an airline loyalty team, this translates to targeted tier-upgrade incentives over blanket discount campaigns.
