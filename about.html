I'm a Business Analytics graduate from the University of Technology Sydney with a focused interest in the commercial and operational dynamics of aviation. My work sits at the intersection of data science and airline economics — translating complex datasets into clear performance insights that commercial teams can actually act on.

My aviation portfolio directly mirrors the work of commercial and flight analyst teams. I've built a predictive airline fare pricing model achieving 98.4% accuracy across six modelling approaches, and I'm developing a route-level demand dashboard using BITRE domestic aviation data to analyse passenger trends and COVID recovery patterns. A third project models operational disruption costs using BTS delay data, framing findings as business recommendations rather than raw statistics.

Where I've worked

Before UTS, I worked as a Data Research Intern at iSchoolConnect Technologies — improving database accuracy by 25% across 500+ institutions, reducing redundant data collection by 40%, and standardising reporting methodology across three international regions. That role taught me something I carry into every analysis: the value of data work is only realised when it reaches a decision-maker in a form they can act on.

In my current role as Shift Leader at Guzman y Gomez, I manage real-time operational performance across a high-volume environment — coordinating staffing, resolving disruptions, and maintaining compliance under time pressure. The operational instincts built there translate directly to airline ground and logistics contexts.

How I think about data

I'm drawn to aviation because it's one of the few industries where data quality has direct safety and commercial consequences. Every route decision, every pricing call, every capacity plan is underpinned by data — and getting that analysis wrong is expensive. That stakes-driven environment is exactly where I want to work.

I'm multilingual across English, Hindi, Marathi, and Nepali, and I bring the same discipline to data that I apply to everything I take seriously. I'm actively targeting commercial analytics roles in the Australian aviation sector, with particular interest in how data can support revenue management, route strategy, and capacity planning decisions.
